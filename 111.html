THE WOODMAN AND THE SERPENT

One wintry day a Woodman was tramping home from his work when
he saw something black lying on the snow. When he came closer he
saw it was a Serpent to all appearance dead. But he took it up and put
it in his bosom to warm while he hurried home. As soon as he got indoors
he put the Serpent down on the hearth before the fire. The children watched
it and saw it slowly come to life again. Then one of them stooped down to
stroke it, but the Serpent raised its head and put out its fangs and was
about to sting the child to death. So the Woodman seized his axe, and with
one stroke cut the Serpent in two. "Ah," said he,

"No gratitude from the wicked."
